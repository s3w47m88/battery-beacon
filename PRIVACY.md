# Battery Beacon — Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** June 10, 2026

Battery Beacon ("the app") is a menu-bar utility for macOS that alerts you when
your Mac's battery reaches a percentage you choose. This policy explains exactly
what the app collects, how it is used, and the choices you have. It is written
to be complete and plain — there is no hidden collection.

## Summary

- Battery Beacon collects a small amount of **anonymous usage analytics** to help
  improve the app.
- It does **not** collect your name, email address, Apple ID, contacts, files,
  battery serial number, or any other personally identifying information.
- Analytics are **not** used for advertising and are **not** linked to your
  identity or used to track you across other apps, products, or websites.
- You can **turn analytics off** at any time in the app's settings.

## What we collect

When analytics are enabled (the default, which you may disable), the app sends
the following anonymous information:

- **An anonymous install identifier** — a random ID (UUID) generated on your
  device. It is not your Apple ID, device serial, or any account identifier, and
  it cannot be used to identify you personally.
- **App usage events** — that the app launched, that a battery alert fired
  (including the threshold percentage and whether the Mac was charging), and that
  the app quit.
- **Basic technical context** — the app version and your macOS version.

Our analytics server records standard web-request metadata when an event is
received — including a coarse, city-level location and device/OS type **derived
from your IP address**. Your IP address itself is used only to derive this
coarse information and is **not stored** with the events.

We do **not** collect: your name, email, phone number, precise location,
contacts, photos, files, browsing history, battery serial numbers, or any data
from other apps.

## How we use it

The information above is used solely to understand how the app is used (for
example, how often alerts fire) so we can fix problems and improve features. It
is **not** sold, **not** shared with advertisers, and **not** used to build an
advertising or cross-app tracking profile.

## Where the data goes

Analytics are sent to a **self-hosted analytics service** operated by the app's
developer at `analytics.theportlandcompany.com` (powered by Umami, a
privacy-focused, cookie-free analytics tool). The data is not provided to any
third-party analytics or advertising network.

## Your choices

- **Opt out:** Open Battery Beacon's settings (click the menu-bar icon →
  Preferences) and turn off **"Share anonymous usage analytics."** When off, the
  app sends nothing.
- Because the data is anonymous, we cannot link it back to you to retrieve or
  delete an individual record. Disabling analytics stops all future collection.

## Data retention

Anonymous, aggregated usage events are retained only as long as needed to
analyze product usage and are not tied to any personal identity.

## Children

Battery Beacon is a general-purpose utility and is not directed at children. It
does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone.
